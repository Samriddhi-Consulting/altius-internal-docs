# ADA & Altius — Copywriter Guidelines (v2)

**The Brand Idea:** "99°C is hot water. 100°C is steam. 1 extra degree makes the difference."
ADA (A Degree Above) believes that small, intentional shifts unlock disproportionate outcomes. Every sentence you write should reflect this discipline. If a word can be removed without losing meaning, it shouldn't be there.

## 1. Voice & Tone
- **Traits:** Quirky · Clever · Professional · Relatable · Short · Human.
- The voice is "low on graphic" and highly functional. It relies on punchy, memorable statements rather than long corporate paragraphs.

## 2. Structural Principles
The copy dictates the design. Short sentences require different layouts than long blocks of text.
- **Short Sentences:** Keep sentences brief. The design uses wide line-heights (1.5–1.6) so short sentences need air to breathe.
- **Punches, Not Blocks:** Deliver copy in punches. Use generous paragraph spacing. Avoid walls of text.
- **Headline Hierarchy:** Write headlines so that the most important word carries the weight. The design will make the most important word the darkest/boldest.

## 3. Microcopy Rules
- **No Filler:** Ban phrases like "Click here" or "Learn more."
- **Specific Verbs:** Use exact, descriptive verbs for all CTAs.
- **CTA verb is locked: "Try a scenario"** — not "Try a demo", "Try a simulation", "Try the demo", or any variation. This is the site-wide standard.
- **Eyebrows:** Eyebrow labels above headlines should be written for `SMALL CAPS`. These fit the editorial tone perfectly.

### 3.1 Altius value loop (benefits, not features)

**Authority:** [altius_shared_blocks.md § Value proposition](../altius_shared_blocks.md) — one-line definition, **tagline**, and **LinkedIn About Us** live there; do not paraphrase.

Altius is **practice + coaching debrief → behaviour change**. Never sell “practice” alone—the debrief is what closes the knowing–doing gap.

- **Tagline** (LinkedIn, pitch decks, explainer headers): *Some lessons you can't afford to learn on the job. Altius™ is how your people don't have to.*

- **Lead with:** outcomes under pressure, behaviour change, coaching feedback, evidence for L&D or accreditation.
- **AI is incidental** in buyer-facing copy (titles, hero, meta, CTAs). Buyers care whether performance changes—not the underlying technology.
- **Say the loop:** briefing → conversation → **coaching debrief** → dashboard/evidence. Step 3 is not optional in positioning.

## 4. The Brand DNA (Δ and °)
The brand uses the Delta (Δ) and Degree (°) symbols natively in text.
- **° (Degree):** Used for bullet points (instead of standard dots) and step markers (e.g., `1°`, `2°`). You can incorporate it into end-of-headline punctuation where conceptually appropriate.
- **Δ (Delta):** Signifies change or the gap.

## 5. Examples of Brand-Aligned Copy
- *"99°C is hot water. 100°C is steam."*
- *"Every Generation. One Team. Relentless."*
- *"Build it, Scale it, Be A Degree Above."*

## 6. The One-Line Rule
"If it's not a degree above, it doesn't belong on this page." Be ruthless in editing.
