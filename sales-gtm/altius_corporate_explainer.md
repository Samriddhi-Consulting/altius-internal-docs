## The Core Idea

People learn to do difficult things by doing difficult things. Not by reading about them. Not by watching someone else do them. Not by passing a quiz.

Think about it. A surgeon practises on a cadaver before operating on a patient. A pilot practises on a simulator before flying a plane. But when it comes to the human side of professional work, we still rely on classroom role-plays, e-learning modules, and hope.

The problem is rarely knowledge. Your people probably *know* what a good discovery question sounds like. They *know* they should listen before pitching. They *know* the SBI model for giving feedback. They just can't do it consistently when the pressure is real, the customer is impatient, and the stakes are high.

That gap between knowing and doing? That's what **Altius** is built for.

Altius is where professionals practise high-stakes conversations, receive structured, behaviour-level coaching feedback the moment they finish, and change how they show up next time. Privately, at their own pace, as many times as they need.

Anywhere there is a human interaction that matters, Altius can simulate it.

## Six Problems You Can't Solve With Classrooms

You already run good programmes. You hire strong facilitators, build sharp content, and your post-training feedback scores are high. But six problems keep coming back.

### 1. The Practice Gap

Your people know the framework. They can't execute it under pressure.

A sales rep learns consultative selling in a workshop. Two weeks later, a customer pushes back on price, and the rep reflexively offers a discount. The workshop didn't fail. The rep just never *practised* handling that pushback in a setting that felt real. Knowledge doesn't transfer to performance without rehearsal. This is well established in learning science, and yet most training programmes skip the rehearsal entirely.

### 2. The Consistency Gap

With 50 or more people across geographies, coaching quality becomes a postcode lottery.

Your best regional manager in Mumbai runs sharp, challenging role-plays. The one in Lucknow means well but lets people off easy. A facilitator in Chennai covers the content; one in Delhi challenges the thinking. Same programme, dramatically different outcomes depending on who delivers it locally. You can't clone your best coaches. You need a system that delivers the same rigour to everyone.

### 3. The Proof Gap

"The training was excellent" is not a business metric.

Leadership asks: *Did the training improve conversion? Can you show me data?* You have smile sheets, attendance registers, and Level 1 feedback. What you don't have is behavioural evidence: how someone actually handled a customer objection, a procurement negotiation, or a difficult conversation with a direct report. Before and after. Measurable. Specific.

### 4. The Scale Problem

Classroom role-plays simply don't work past 30 people.

When you have 50, 200, or 500 people in a function spread across the country, running individual, high-quality practice sessions with each person is logistically impossible. You end up compromising. Group exercises where half the room disengages. Or you skip practice altogether and hope the content sticks. It usually doesn't.

### 5. The Embarrassment Problem

This one is real, and it's bigger than most L&D teams acknowledge.

Adults don't want to role-play in front of peers. The social risk kills practice quality. A mid-career sales manager won't fumble through a tough objection in front of colleagues. They'll default to something smooth and generic. The practice looks polished, but nobody actually stretched.

Altius removes the audience. Every simulation is private. Just the learner and the AI counterpart. People try harder, fail more honestly, and learn faster when nobody is watching.

### 6. The Relevance Problem

Every team thinks "our conversations are different." And they're right.

Generic communication training doesn't land with a procurement team, a quality auditor, or a government liaison officer. They sit through the workshop, mentally filing it under "not for me." The scenarios need to reflect their stakeholders, their pressure points, their language. Altius scenarios are custom-built for each context, because a sales objection and a vendor audit and a feedback conversation are three completely different muscles.

## What Altius Does

Altius gives your people a realistic AI counterpart to practise with, and gives you granular data on how each person performed. The experience has four steps.

### Step 1: The Briefing

The learner receives a scenario link via email. They read a contextual briefing, the way you'd prep before a real meeting:

> *"You're about to meet Vikram, the fleet operations head at a logistics company. He's unhappy about delayed spare parts and is considering switching to a competitor. Your goal: retain the account."*

No app to install. No LMS to navigate. Click the link, read the brief, start.

### Step 2: The Conversation

The learner chats with an AI-powered persona. Not a chatbot. Not a script. A character with a backstory, a personality, hidden concerns, emotional triggers, and responses that adapt in real time.

If the learner asks sharp questions, the persona opens up gradually. If they jump straight to a pitch, the persona gets guarded. If they show empathy, the persona softens. If they get pushy, the persona pushes back.

The AI responds in one to two seconds. The conversation runs for 15 to 25 exchanges, calibrated to the scenario, simulating the natural arc of a real interaction.

It feels like messaging a real person. That's the point.

### Step 3: The Coaching Debrief

The moment the conversation ends, the system generates a structured coaching evaluation. Not pass/fail. A nuanced, evidence-backed assessment.

| What's Measured | Example |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Strengths** | "Strong rapport-building in the opening. Used the counterpart's own language to reframe the problem." |
| **Areas to Improve** | "Missed the hidden concern about switching costs. Moved to solution before fully understanding the situation." |
| **Behaviour Scores** | Active Listening: 4/5 · Empathy: 3/5 · Needs Discovery: 2/5 · Solution Framing: 4/5 |
| **Overall Assessment** | "Competent with room to grow. Recommend focused practice on probing questions." |

The rubric is custom-built for your scenario, measuring the exact behaviours your organisation cares about.

### Step 4: Your Dashboard

As the L&D leader, HRBP, or functional head, you see operational progress.

- Who has completed the simulation and who hasn't.
- Full transcripts. You can drill into any person's conversation for coaching QA.
- The scored coaching debrief and behaviour scores sit with the specialist path (ADA, or someone on your team we have trained), not in the corporate admin dashboard.
- Cohort-level patterns still land for the programme. If 80% of the team scored low on needs discovery, you know exactly what to focus the next workshop on, from the specialist analysis and cohort report.

## How We Set This Up

This is what happens after you say yes: the operator path from first brief to cohort evidence. (The four steps above are the learner session.)

1. **Brief.** You name one conversation that matters: who the counterpart is, what is at stake, and which behaviours you want assessed. What do your best people do that average performers don't?
2. **Design.** We build the persona, the learner briefing, the scene rules, and the rubric. Usually days, not a platform project. You don't write prompts. You don't configure an LMS.
3. **Review.** You (and a line SME if you want one) play-test. We iterate until the pushback feels real and the scoring matches what "good" looks like in your organisation. Live in one to two weeks from a clear brief.
4. **Cohort.** We stand up your organisation space and invite the pilot group by email. Magic link. Browser on phone or laptop. No app. No LMS required.
5. **Run.** People practise asynchronously. You see who has completed and can open transcripts for coaching QA.
6. **Debrief & evidence.** An L&D specialist (ADA, or someone on your team we have trained) runs the coaching debrief from the Altius analysis: transcript, behaviour scores, what to fix next. You leave with individual scorecards and a cohort summary leadership can actually read.

A typical first run is 20 to 30 people on one scenario.
