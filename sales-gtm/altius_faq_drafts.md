## `/corporate` — 16 questions

### Section 1: Why training doesn't change behaviour

#### C-U1 — Why doesn't corporate training change how people behave under pressure?

Most corporate training is built to transfer knowledge: frameworks, models, compliance content, slide decks. That works for what people _know_. It rarely works for what people _do_ when a customer pushes back, a deadline slips, or a direct report gets defensive.

The pattern has a name and a literature: Jeffrey Pfeffer and Robert Sutton's knowing-doing gap (Harvard Business School Press, 2000). Learning transfer research since has confirmed the same thing repeatedly: most training improves knowledge. Sustained behaviour change on the job is less common, and the gap traces almost always to one cause: no deliberate rehearsal in conditions that feel real. Altius™ is built for that second step: the practice conversation and the structured coaching debrief that follows. That is how knowing becomes doing.

---

#### C-U3 — Why do sales reps know the discovery framework but still jump to pitch in live calls?

Because knowing the framework and executing it when a buyer is impatient, sceptical, or mid-crisis are different skills. A rep can explain SPIN® Selling (Huthwaite International) or consultative selling frameworks in a classroom. On a live call, _the instinct to relieve tension often wins_: offer a discount, lead with the deck, skip to the product.

The workshop did not necessarily fail. The rep may never have _practised_ handling that pushback in conditions that felt real. Altius™ gives reps a private space to run those conversations repeatedly, with a persona that responds the way buyers actually do, and a debrief that shows exactly where they jumped to pitch.

---

#### C-P1 — Why do managers avoid difficult feedback conversations even after training?

Managers often leave training knowing the model: SBI (Center for Creative Leadership), feedforward frameworks, whatever your programme uses. The gap is not the model. It is the muscle: staying calm, leading with observation, letting the other person respond, holding silence when it gets uncomfortable.

_That muscle is hard to build in a group workshop_ where everyone is performing for the room. Many managers avoid the conversation entirely, or rush through it to end the discomfort. Altius™ lets managers practise feedback and performance conversations privately, with realistic pushback, and receive behaviour-level coaching on what they actually said, not what they intended to say.

---

#### C-P2 — Why does classroom role-play stop working once a team grows past 30 people?

High-quality role-play needs time, attention, and a skilled facilitator for each participant. _Past roughly 30 people, the maths breaks down_: you move to group exercises where half the room watches, or you skip practice and hope the content sticks.

_Across geographies, quality becomes a postcode lottery_. Your best manager in one city runs sharp role-plays; another city gets a polite walk-through. Altius™ delivers the same rigorous AI counterpart and rubric to every learner at once, without flying facilitators to eight cities.

---

#### C-P3 — Why do adults underperform in peer role-plays, even when they know the framework?

Social risk. A mid-career sales manager will not fumble through a tough objection in front of colleagues. They default to something smooth and generic. The exercise looks polished; nobody actually stretched.

Altius™ removes the audience: each simulation is between the learner and the AI persona. People try harder, _fail more honestly_, and learn faster when peers are not in the room.

---

#### C-P4 — How do L&D leaders prove to leadership that training improved performance, beyond smile sheets and attendance?

Leadership asks a fair question: _Did this change how people perform?_ Attendance registers and Level 1 feedback cannot answer it. What you need is behavioural evidence: how someone actually handled an objection, a negotiation, or a difficult conversation with a direct report, not just whether they attended.

Altius™ produces _transcript-backed coaching debriefs with per-criterion behaviour scores against a rubric built for your context_. You can show individual improvement, cohort patterns ("80% scored low on needs discovery"), and exportable summaries for your CFO. That is **evidence of doing, not just of showing up**.

### Section 2: How Altius is different

#### C-S1 — How is Altius different from e-learning or a standard LMS module?

E-learning and LMS modules are strong at distributing content: videos, quizzes, completion tracking. They are passive. Finishing a module is not the same as handling a live customer or a defensive stakeholder.

Altius™ is active practice. The learner has a two-way conversation with a persona that adapts in real time, then receives a coaching debrief on behaviour, not just recall. You do not need to replace your LMS; you add the practice layer that modules cannot provide. Learners access simulations via a browser link; no app install required.

---

#### C-S2 — What happens after the simulation: is there a coaching debrief or just a score?

A full coaching debrief, delivered by an L&D specialist. That is the point.

When the conversation ends, Altius™ generates a structured analysis: the full transcript, per-criterion behaviour scores against your rubric, and an evidence-based account of what the learner said, where the AI persona pushed back, and what was missed. That analysis goes to the L&D specialist, not to the learner as an automated display.

The specialist (from ADA, or a member of your own team whom ADA has trained) uses that analysis to deliver a personalised debrief to each participant: what landed, what missed, what to work on next. The AI produces the evidence; the specialist provides the coaching judgment that turns it into behaviour change.

For large cohorts, ADA works with your L&D team to build internal debrief capability so the process scales without depending on ADA consultants on every run. Your specialists learn to read the Altius™ analysis and conduct debriefs independently.

---

#### C-S3 — Can Altius scenarios be customised for our industry, our stakeholders, and our rubric?

Yes. Every scenario is built for your context: the counterpart type, the stakes, the language, and the behaviours you want to measure. You describe the conversation and what "good" looks like; we design the persona, briefing, and rubric. You review and we iterate.

Scenarios are modular: persona, scene, briefing, and rubric are self-contained, so new simulations for different teams or industries can be designed and deployed in days, not months. Generic communication training fails because procurement, quality, and sales conversations are different muscles. Altius™ treats them that way.

---

#### C-S4 — Is the AI persona just a chatbot, or does it actually react to how I handle the conversation?

It reacts. That is the product. The persona has a backstory, hidden concerns, and emotional triggers. It responds to what you say, how you say it, and what you leave out.

If the learner asks sharp questions, the persona opens up. If they jump to a pitch, it gets guarded. If they show empathy, it softens. If they get pushy, it pushes back. The conversation runs 15–25 exchanges, long enough for a real dynamic. It is designed to feel like messaging a real person, because practice only works if the pressure feels real.

In a live deployment with a leading Indian automotive OEM, 40 managers practised with an AI fleet operator who was unhappy, under pressure, and weighing a switch to a competitor. The conversations required empathy, probing, and commercial creativity. Each manager's choices produced a different dialogue. The best way to see how the persona behaves is to run one. ### Section 3: Getting started

#### C-D1 — How long does it take to deploy an Altius simulation from brief to live?

Typically **one to two weeks** from brief to live scenario. You provide a description of the conversation, the counterpart, and the behaviours you want assessed; we build the persona, briefing, and rubric. You review; we iterate until it is right.

---

#### C-D2 — Does Altius require an LMS integration or an app install?

No. Learners need a phone or laptop with a browser. They receive a magic link by email, read the briefing, and start. No app download. No LMS integration required, though we can discuss it if your organisation wants one.

---

#### C-D3 — How is Altius priced: per user, per cohort, or platform licence?

**Per-scenario, per-cohort.** There is no platform licence and no multi-year lock-in required. You pay for the simulations you run with the cohorts you care about, not for shelfware. Annual arrangements are available if you prefer them, but they are not the default model.

---

#### C-D4 — Can a learner redo the simulation? Is repetition built in?

That depends on the programme design, and we have a recommendation.

Behaviour rarely changes in a single pass. On the same scenario, we suggest two to three attempts with a specialist debrief between each: the debrief shows what shifted; the next attempt applies it. That cycle is what makes the behaviour reliable, not just improved once.

From there, the progression moves across scenarios: a different counterpart, a harder conversation type, a new stakeholder. Each with a debrief. That breadth is what builds a transferable capability. How many attempts and scenarios to build in is something we design at the outset with your L&D team, not something left to learner discretion on the day.

---

#### C-D5 — Is each simulation private, and can peers or managers see a participant's transcript?

During practice, there is no audience of peers watching a role-play. That is the psychological safety difference. The conversation is between the learner and the AI persona.

Organisation administrators (L&D, HRBP, functional leads you designate) can access transcripts and debriefs through the dashboard for coaching, quality assurance, and reporting. Access is organisation-isolated; you see only your team's data. You control who has admin visibility. Peers do not watch each other practise live.

## `/education` — 19 questions

### Section 1: The gap in professional education

#### E-U1 — Why can students pass exams on communication or selling but still struggle in real conversations?

Because exams and assignments test what students can _explain_: frameworks, definitions, structured answers. A sceptical customer, a defensive employee, or an anxious patient does not reward textbook language.

Put students in a live conversation and many freeze, default to jargon, or jump to a solution before they understand the problem. They were taught well. They were rarely asked to _do_ the conversation under realistic pressure. Altius™ closes that gap: practise, debrief, repeat.

---

#### E-U2 — What does the employability gap look like in practice, and what do recruiters say is missing?

Placement cells hear it every year. Graduates know theory but struggle in professional conversations: discovery calls, client meetings, feedback sessions, negotiations. Recruiters want application under pressure, not just framework fluency on a CV.

Students spend hundreds of hours preparing for exams. They spend almost no time practising the interactions they will have in their first job. The gap shows up in interviews, internships, and early performance reviews. The marksheet does not catch it.

---

#### E-U3 — How do I assess 60 students on communication skills without running 60 individual role-plays?

You cannot. Not with integrity, not in the time you have. Even at ten minutes per student, one exercise is ten hours of facilitation plus feedback. Group role-plays mean only two students practise while others watch, and the "customer" is another student who cannot push back convincingly.

Altius™ runs an adaptive, private conversation with every student in the same week. Each gets a 15–25 exchange simulation and a detailed coaching debrief. You review aggregate patterns ("32 of 58 scored below 3 on probing depth") and drill into individual transcripts when you need to, without running the room yourself.

---

#### E-P1 — What is CO/PO attainment evidence for soft skills, and why are written exams insufficient?

Outcome-Based Education requires you to show students _attained_ stated outcomes, not just sat through a course. For technical subjects, exams work. For outcomes like _"conduct a consultative sales conversation"_ or _"deliver constructive feedback,"_ written exams test whether students can _describe_ the skill, not _do_ it.

CO/PO attainment for soft skills needs direct assessment of behaviour under pressure. Altius™ maps rubric criteria to your Course Outcomes; the percentage of students above a threshold on those criteria becomes your attainment metric: auditable, timestamped, and individual.

---

#### E-P2 — Why doesn't the case method develop students' ability to hold a real professional conversation?

Students learn to think _about_ a conversation. The case method rarely teaches them to _have_ one.

Cases develop analytical thinking well. The customer in the case does not push back when the student pitches too early. The CEO does not get defensive. The vendor does not change position based on what the student says.

Altius™ is the layer that comes after: the briefing reads like a case, then the stakeholder talks back, adapting to what the student does in real time.

---

#### E-P3 — Why is internship evidence often thin when accreditation panels review experiential learning?

Most SSR folders show participation: attendance logs, certificates, supervisor sign-offs. Panels increasingly ask whether experiential learning produced _competence_, not just exposure.

Internships are realistic but uncontrolled; evidence is often a subjective rating or a completion letter. Altius™ adds standardised, scored, transcript-backed assessment of a realistic professional interaction: rigour closer to an exam, realism closer to the workplace.

---

#### E-P4 — What pedagogical innovation can an institution credibly claim if it deploys Altius?

You can credibly claim **simulation-based, direct assessment of professional conversational competence**, with evidence panels can actually inspect. Not a buzzword in the Self-Study Report, but timestamped transcripts, per-criterion behaviour scores, and cohort analytics mapped to your Course Outcomes.

That sits between exams (rigorous but artificial) and internships (realistic but unstandardised). For NAAC, NBA, NEP, and international frameworks, it supports narratives around experiential learning, outcome-based assessment, and industry-ready graduates, with artefacts IQAC can actually file.

### Section 2: Pedagogy, assessment, and accreditation

#### E-S1 — Is Altius a replacement for the case method?

No. It complements it. Cases develop analytical thinking; Altius™ develops conversational competence. The student reads a contextual briefing (like a case), then enters a live conversation where the stakeholder responds to what they do.

Use cases to teach students to analyse the decision. Use Altius™ to make them _make_ it: under pressure, with pushback, with consequences in the dialogue.

---

#### E-S2 — How does Altius differ from a viva voce or group presentation for assessing soft skills?

A viva tests articulation in a controlled, faculty-dependent setting, hard to standardise across examiners.

Presentations are followed by panels that question and probe. But a panel can only engage with what was presented to them. They cannot introduce what the student was never given: a hidden concern the student has to uncover, a persona who becomes guarded when pitched too early, or an emotional register that shifts based on what the student does and says.

Altius™ brings that dimension in. It is a two-way, adaptive conversation in which the student discovers information through what they ask, not just through what they present. Scoring is behaviour-level against defined criteria, consistent for every student. Every student faces the same scenario and the same standard, with a full transcript for faculty moderation.

---

#### E-S3 — How does Altius help generate NAAC accreditation evidence for experiential learning?

NAAC Criterion 2 (Teaching-Learning and Evaluation) and Criterion 5 (Student Support and Progression) expect evidence of innovative pedagogy and experiential learning, including outcomes, not just activity logs.

Altius™ provides simulation-based assessment with **timestamped transcripts**, per-student behaviour scores, qualitative coaching feedback, and cohort analytics. That documents both that experiential learning occurred and what it produced: a stronger category of proof than attendance sheets or project cover pages alone.

#### E-S5 — Is the AI persona just a chatbot, or does it adapt to how the learner handles the conversation?

It adapts continuously, responding to each student's choices rather than matching preset keywords. The persona has a role, backstory, and hidden concerns. If a student acknowledges a crisis and probes root causes, the persona opens up. If they ignore context and pitch, it stays closed.

That is what makes it the next layer after case studies: the case study talks back. The conversation runs 15–25 exchanges, long enough for a real dynamic and short enough for realistic time pressure in a course schedule.

In a pilot with a business school cohort, students practised with Kabir Mehta, a CPO mid-crisis: customs hold, a Diwali deadline, 70% of his attention on firefighting. Students who acknowledged the crisis before asking about the product found a persona who opened up; students who led with a pitch found one who stayed closed and said so. **94% rated the experience good or excellent**; **8 in 10** said it deepened their understanding more than a lecture alone. The best way to see how it behaves is to run a scenario. [Try a scenario →](/demos)

#### E-S6 — Does every student get a unique conversation, or can they look up what someone else said?

Every conversation is unique because the persona _responds_ to what each student says, how they say it, and what they leave out. There is no fixed script to memorise or share as "the right answers."

Students cannot look up a classmate's path and replay it; the dialogue branches on their choices. For integrity, treat Altius™ like any authentic assessment: design rubrics that reward genuine probing and reflection, combine with your own domain grading where needed, and use timestamps and transcripts for moderation. Faculty remain the authority on professional accuracy in law, medicine, and other regulated disciplines; Altius™ assesses _how_ students communicate.

### Section 3: Implementation

#### E-D1 — How much faculty time is required to set up and run an Altius simulation?

**Setup:** You describe the conversation, counterpart, and competences to assess, typically in one collaborative brief. We design the persona, briefing, and rubric; you review and iterate. Live in **one to two weeks**.

**During the run:** Students complete simulations asynchronously. You monitor completion and cohort patterns on the dashboard: less time grading individual performances, more time using aggregate insights to teach ("most of the batch struggled on probing depth").

---

#### E-D2 — Do students need to install an app or log into our LMS to use Altius?

No app install. Students use a browser on phone or laptop. They receive a magic link by email, read the briefing, and start. LMS integration is not required, though we can discuss it if your institution wants single sign-on or grade passback later.

---

#### E-D3 — What does the accreditation evidence actually look like, and what goes into the SSR or IQAC folder?

Per student, Altius™ generates:

- A full **conversation transcript** (timestamped messages)
- **Per-criterion behaviour scores** from the scenario rubric
- **Overall score**, qualitative **summary**, **strengths**, and **areas to improve**
- **Session start and completion timestamps**

For attainment, you apply your threshold (e.g. ≥3/5 on mapped criteria) and calculate the percentage of students who meet it per Course Outcome, using the standard OBE formula. The evidence bundle for moderators typically includes score exports, sample transcripts, and the CO–rubric mapping table you define when you set up the scenario. See [ACCREDITATION_EVIDENCE.md](./ACCREDITATION_EVIDENCE.md) for field-level detail.

---

#### E-D4 — Can Altius be used as a graded assignment, or is it only for practice?

Both. Many programmes use it as a **graded assignment**: the simulation is the assessment; the debrief supplies formative detail; overall and criterion scores feed your gradebook. Others use it as low-stakes practice before a viva or live presentation.

You set the weighting, the threshold, and the number of attempts. The platform does not impose a limit; faculty decide whether students have one attempt or several. For graded use, most faculty choose a single attempt, with separate ungraded practice runs available beforehand to prepare students for the real thing. Faculty can review transcripts for moderation the same way they would review exam scripts.

---

#### E-D5 — What does the student experience look like from start to finish?

1. **Briefing:** Student opens the link and reads a contextual scenario brief (like prep before a real meeting).
2. **Conversation:** 15–25 exchanges with an adaptive AI persona in a chat interface.
3. **Analysis:** The simulation ends. Altius™ generates a structured analysis: transcript, per-criterion behaviour scores, strengths, and areas to develop. This goes to the faculty member or specialist, not as an automated display to the student.
4. **Debrief:** Faculty or the programme specialist reviews the analysis and delivers a personalised coaching debrief to each student. Completion and the full analysis are visible to faculty via the admin view; exports are available for programme reporting.

No passwords to remember (magic-link access). Typical session fits a class period or homework window.
